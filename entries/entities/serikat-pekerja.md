# Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama

## Overview

Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama (SPPMB) is the officially recognized workers union representing approximately 1,150 production and support staff at PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing. The union was formally established in 2005 and has maintained constructive labor relations with management.

**Union Details:**
- **Established:** March 15, 2005
- **Membership:** ~1,150 (96% of eligible workforce)
- **Chairperson:** Bambang Supriyadi (elected 2020, term through 2026)
- **Headquarters:** On-site office at [[pabrik-cimahi]]

## Key Negotiated Agreements

### 2023 Collective Labor Agreement (PKB)
- **Wage Increase:** 8.5% (above mandated minimum wage increase of 6.2%)
- **Profit Sharing:** 5% of net income distributed to workers (up from 3.5%)
- **Upskilling Commitment:** IDR 2.8B training budget (see [[keputusan-2023]])
- **Job Security:** No layoffs due to [[mesin-tenun-otomatis]] deployment

### Health & Safety Wins
- COVID-19 protocols ([[pivot-ppe-2020]]) — zero workplace transmissions
- Ergonomic workstation upgrades at both factories
- Mental health support program launched 2022

## Relationship with Management

**Positive Working Relationship:**
- Regular monthly meetings with [[ratna-sari]] (HR Director)
- Collaborative approach to [[mesin-tenun-otomatis]] implementation
- Joint oversight of upskilling program

**Areas of Tension:**
- **Dividend Policy ([[keputusan-2023]]):** Union opposed reduction to 25%, felt workers deserved higher profit-sharing
- **Surabaya Expansion:** Concerned about resource diversion from West Java operations
- **Contract Workers:** Union pushing to convert 150 contract workers to permanent status (ongoing negotiation)

## Major Strikes/Actions

- **2010:** 2-day strike over wage disputes (resolved with 12% increase)
- **2018:** Work-to-rule action during ERP implementation (resolved in 3 days)
- **2020-present:** No strikes; collaborative approach during COVID-19 and automation
