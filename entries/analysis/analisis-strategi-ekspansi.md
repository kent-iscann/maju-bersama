# Analisis Strategi Ekspansi Surabaya (Surabaya Expansion Strategy Analysis)

## Overview

This analysis evaluates PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing's decision to establish a third production facility in Surabaya, East Java, approved on June 10, 2023 (see [[keputusan-2023]]). The decision passed 4-1, with [[sri-wahyuni]] (CFO) dissenting on cash flow concerns.

**Decision Summary:**
- **Investment:** IDR 85 billion
- **Target Completion:** Q4 2024
- **Initial Capacity:** 1.5 million meters fabric/year, 350 employees
- **Vote:** 4-1 (Sri Wahyuni opposed)

## Strategic Rationale (Proponents: [[budiarjo-widodo]], [[lina-susanti]])

### 1. Market Access
- **Surabaya Port:** Closer to major export shipping routes (vs Bandung→Jakarta→Export)
- **Domestic Market:** East Java is Indonesia's 2nd largest textile market (18% of domestic demand)
- **Supply Chain:** Proximity to cotton traders and textile supply chain in East Java

### 2. Cost Advantages
- **Labor Costs:** East Java ~15% lower than West Java (IDR 3.2M vs 3.8M/month average)
- **Land Costs:** Industrial land IDR 1.8M/m² (Surabaya) vs IDR 2.6M/m² (Bandung)
- **Utilities:** 8% lower electricity rates in East Java

### 3. Risk Diversification
- **Geographic Risk:** Reduces reliance on West Java (natural disasters, labor unrest)
- **Capacity Expansion:** Current facilities at 92% utilization (near capacity constraint)
- **Competitive Positioning:** Competitors (Sritex, Pan Brothers) already have East Java presence

## Counterarguments ([[sri-wahyuni]], CFO)

### 1. Cash Flow Strain
- **2023 Commitments:** IDR 147.5B total capex (Surabaya 85B + Solar 12B + Automation 30B + ERP 3.5B + Other 17B)
- **Debt-to-Equity:** Projected rise from 0.42x (2022) to 0.68x (2024)
- **Liquidity:** Current ratio projected to drop to 1.2x (industry min: 1.5x)

### 2. Execution Risks
- **Greenfield vs Acquisition:** Management chose greenfield (longer time to production)
- **Talent Gap:** Shortage of skilled technicians in East Java textile sector
- **Cultural Differences:** West Java management style may not translate to East Java workforce

### 3. Opportunity Cost
- **Alternative:** Expand [[pabrik-lembang]] (existing facility, lower risk)
- **Alternative:** Acquire smaller competitor (faster market entry, existing workforce)

## Comparative Analysis: Greenfield vs Acquisition

| Factor | Greenfield (Chosen) | Acquisition (Alternative) |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Time to Production | 18 months | 3-6 months |
| Cost | IDR 85B | IDR 110-130B (acquisition premium) |
| Control | Full design control | Retrofit constraints |
| Risk | Execution risk | Integration risk (culture, systems) |
| Customization | Purpose-built for [[mesin-tenun-otomatis]] | Retrofit costs likely IDR 20B+ |

**Assessment:** Greenfield was correct choice given company's technology roadmap (automation-focused).

## Financial Impact Analysis

### Revenue Projection (Conservative)
- **Year 1 (2025):** IDR 45B revenue (60% utilization)
- **Year 2 (2026):** IDR 85B revenue (90% utilization)
- **Year 3 (2027):** IDR 110B revenue (full utilization)
- **Breakeven:** Q3 2026 (end of Year 2)

### ROI Calculation
- **Investment:** IDR 85B
- **5-Year NPV:** IDR 42B (discount rate 12%)
- **IRR:** 18.5% (above company hurdle rate of 15%)
- **Payback Period:** 4.2 years

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation (from [[keputusan-2023]]) |
|------|-------------|--------|--------------------------------------|
| Construction delays | Medium | High | Fixed-price contract with Surabaya builder |
| Labor shortages | High | Medium | [[ratna-sari]] leading East Java recruitment drive |
| Market downturn | Low | High | Phased build (Phase 1: 50% capacity) |
| Cash flow strain | Medium | High | [[sri-wahyuni]] monitoring; IDR 30B cash buffer maintained |

## Decision Quality Assessment

### Score: 7.5/10

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Strong strategic rationale (market access, cost savings)
- ✅ Greenfield choice aligns with automation strategy
- ✅ Positive ROI (18.5% IRR)
- ✅ Supports [[divisi-ekspor]] growth (port proximity)

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ High debt load (0.68x debt-to-equity)
- ❌ Thin liquidity buffer (1.2x current ratio)
- ❌ Execution risk (greenfield, new geography)
- ⚠️ [[sri-wahyuni]]'s dissent was valid (cash flow concerns)

## Recommendation

**Proceed with monitoring:**
1. Phase construction (reduce upfront cash outlay)
2. Secure additional banking partner (reduce reliance on [[pt-bank-mandiri]])
3. Accelerate [[divisi-ekspor]] sales to fill capacity quickly
4. Monthly cash flow reviews ([[sri-wahyuni]] to lead)
