# Strategi Omni-Channel (Omni-Channel Strategy)

## Overview

Strategi Omni-Channel is PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing's strategy to integrate multiple sales channels (B2B, B2C, e-commerce, direct export) into a seamless customer experience. Launched in 2021 as part of [[transformasi-digital]], the strategy has underperformed vs targets but shows promise in specific areas.

**Strategy Details:**
- **Launch:** 2021 (part of [[transformasi-digital]])
- **Investment:** IDR 5 billion (platform development + marketing)
- **Target (2023):** 20% direct sales (actual: 5%)
- **Led by:** [[heryanto-prasetyo]] (CTO) + [[divisi-ekspor]] team

## Channel Mix (2023 Actual vs Target)

| Channel | Revenue (IDR B) | % Actual | % Target | Status |
|---------|-----------------|----------|----------|--------|
| Traditional B2B (brands) | 272 | 80% | 60% | ✅ Overperforming |
| Direct Export (e-commerce) | 10.2 | 3% | 20% | ❌ Underperforming |
| Domestic E-commerce | 6.8 | 2% | 10% | ❌ Underperforming |
| Company Website (D2C) | 3.4 | 1% | 5% | ❌ Underperforming |
| Marketplace (Tokopedia/Shopee) | 47.6 | 14% | 5% | ✅ Overperforming |

**Key Insight:** Marketplaces (Tokopedia/Shopee) vastly outperform company-owned channels.

## Why Strategy Underperformed

### 1. Brand Awareness (or lack thereof)
- **Issue:** PT Maju Bersama is B2B brand; consumers don't know us
- **Impact:** Company website (D2C) only IDR 3.4B (1% of revenue)
- **Solution Attempted:** IDR 2B digital marketing campaign (2022) → Failed (0.5% conversion)

### 2. Platform Strategy Error
- **Mistake:** Built own e-commerce platform (IDR 3B investment)
- **Reality:** Customers prefer Tokopedia, Shopee, Alibaba
- **Pivot (2023):** Partnered with platforms → Success! (IDR 47.6B via marketplaces)

### 3. B2B Dependency
- **Strength:** Strong B2B relationships ([[divisi-ekspor]] with Gap, Uniqlo, Toyota)
- **Weakness:** Hard to shift to D2C when you're a "throat" supplier (not consumer brand)
- **Reality Check:** 80% B2B is not a failure; it's our core competency

## Successful Pivot: Marketplace Partnerships (2023)

### Domestic (Tokopedia, Shopee)
- **"Maju Wear" Brand:** Casual wear, workwear
- **Revenue (2023):** IDR 34B (10% of domestic market)
- **Growth:** +120% YoY (from IDR 15.5B in 2022)
- **Strategy:** Use platforms' logistics + marketing (faster than自建)

### Export (Alibaba, Made-in-China.com)
- **B2B Marketplace:** Selling fabric by the roll (not finished garments)
- **Revenue (2023):** IDR 13.6B (4% of export, new channel)
- **Customers:** Small garment makers in USA, EU, Japan
- **Growth:** +200% YoY (from IDR 4.5B in 2022)

## Revised Strategy (2024-2026)

### 1. Don't Fight B2B Gravity
- **Accept:** 70-80% B2B is healthy ([[divisi-ekspor]] relationships are gold)
- **Focus:** Deepen B2B relationships (Toyota, Uniqlo, Gap) via API integration ([[keputusan-2023]] ERP)

### 2. Marketplace-First for D2C
- **Domestic:** Expand on Tokopedia/Shopee (target: IDR 60B by 2025)
- **Export:** Alibaba, Made-in-China for B2B fabric sales
- **Company Website:** Downgrade to "brochure-ware" (cost reduction)

### 3. [[cetak-batik-digital]] Custom Orders
- **Opportunity:** Direct-to-consumer custom batik ([[batik-tradisional]] + [[cetak-batik-digital]])
- **Integration:** Website for design → print → ship (automated workflow)
- **Target:** IDR 15B by 2025 (niche, high-margin)

## Financial Impact (2023)

| Channel | ROI | Assessment |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| Traditional B2B | 25% IRR | ✅ Core business, keep investing |
| Marketplaces | 18% IRR | ✅ Growing fast, increase allocation |
| Company Website D2C | -5% IRR | ❌ Shut down or pivot to brochure |
| B2B E-commerce (Alibaba) | 12% IRR | ✅ Promising, scale up |

## Strategic Assessment: 6/10

**Why so low?**
- ❌ Built own platform (IDR 3B waste)
- ❌ Overestimated D2C potential (brand awareness issue)
- ❌ Target miss: 5% vs 20% direct sales

**But there's hope:**
- ✅ Marketplace pivot working (IDR 47.6B, +200% growth)
- ✅ B2B marketplace (Alibaba) showing promise
- ✅ [[cetak-batik-digital]] custom orders emerging
