# Ratna Sari

## Overview

Ratna Sari is the Human Resources Director of PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing, appointed in 2018. She manages workforce planning, talent development, and labor relations, including the company's relationship with [Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama](serikat-pekerja.md) (Workers Union).

**Personal Details:**
- **Born:** January 28, 1980, in Yogyakarta, Indonesia
- **Education:**
  - B.Sc. Psychology, Universitas Gadjah Mada, 2002
  - M.Sc. Human Resource Management, University of Melbourne, 2008
- **Previous Experience:**
  - PT Astra International, HR Manager (2008-2014)
  - PT Unilever Indonesia, HR Director (2014-2018)

## Role in Company

Ratna oversees:
- **Workforce Planning** — Recruitment, retention, succession planning
- **Talent Development** — Upskilling programs, leadership development
- **Labor Relations** — Primary liaison with [Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama](serikat-pekerja.md)
- **Compensation & Benefits** — Salary structures, profit-sharing, incentives

### Key Initiatives

#### Upskilling Program ([Key Decisions 2023 — PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing](keputusan-2023.md))
Designed and implemented IDR 2.8B worker training program:
- **Goal:** Prepare 500 workers for increased automation ([Sistem Tenun Otomatis (Automated Weaving System)](mesin-tenun-otomatis.md))
- **Curriculum:** Machine operation, basic maintenance, quality control
- **Status:** 500 workers trained as of December 2023
- **Result:** Zero layoffs due to automation; 95% retention rate

#### Worker Welfare During COVID-19 ([Pivot PPE 2020 — COVID-19 Response Case Study](pivot-ppe-2020.md))
Led health and safety protocols during PPE production pivot:
- Zero COVID-19 cases among workforce (128 diagnosed cases, all remote)
- Implemented shift rotations, temperature checks, sanitization stations
- Provided housing allowance for workers avoiding public transport

## Labor Relations Philosophy

"Workers are partners, not costs. Every automation decision must include a human development plan." — Led to innovative profit-sharing model where workers receive 5% of automation cost savings annually.

### Notable Position: Solar Energy Investment ([Key Decisions 2023 — PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing](keputusan-2023.md))
Voted against solar panel investment (March 2023):
- **Concern:** Labor reallocation during installation (temporary disruption)
- **Alternative:** Phased installation during low-production months
- **Outcome:** Decision passed 4-1; compromise reached with phased installation schedule
