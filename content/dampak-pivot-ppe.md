# Dampak Pivot PPE (Impact Analysis: COVID-19 PPE Pivot)

## Overview

This analysis evaluates the long-term impact of PT Maju Bersama Manufacturing's 2020 pivot to PPE production ([Pivot PPE 2020 — COVID-19 Response Case Study](pivot-ppe-2020.md)). While the pivot saved the company during the pandemic, its post-pandemic legacy is mixed.

**PPE Timeline:**
- **2020:** Emergency pivot, IDR 28B revenue (12% of total)
- **2021:** Peak demand, IDR 51B revenue (18% of total)
- **2022:** Market normalization begins, IDR 44.7B (15% of total)
- **2023:** Post-pandemic reality, IDR 17B (5% of total)

## Financial Impact Analysis

### The Pandemic Lifeline (2020-2021)

| Metric | Without PPE (projected) | With PPE (actual) | Impact |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Revenue (2020) | IDR 220B (-35% vs 2019) | IDR 245B (-9% vs 2019) | +IDR 25B |
| Net Income (2020) | IDR 15B | IDR 22B | +46.7% |
| Jobs Saved | Layoffs: ~400 workers | Zero layoffs | 400 families |
| Cash Burn | IDR 28B → bankruptcy risk | IDR 45B (surplus) | Survival |

**Assessment:** ✅ Pivot was a lifesaver (literally and figuratively)

### The Post-Pandemic Overhang (2022-2023)

| Metric | 2022 | 2023 | Trend |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| PPE Revenue | IDR 44.7B | IDR 17B | -62% ❌ |
| Capacity Utilization | 85% | 60% | -25% ❌ |
| Inventory Write-off | IDR 3.2B | IDR 5.8B | +81% ❌ |
| Opportunity Cost | Medium | High | Getting worse ❌ |

**Critical Issue:** 2.4 million units/year capacity, only 60% utilized (2023) = IDR 8B+ in sunk costs.

## Strategic Options Analysis (2024-2026)

### Option 1: Exit PPE (Shut Down Line)
- **Pros:** Stop losses, free up factory space ([Pabrik Cimahi](pabrik-cimahi.md))
- **Cons:** Write off IDR 12B equipment, 200 workers displaced
- **[Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama](serikat-pekerja.md) Impact:** High risk (union opposition)
- **Score:** 4/10

### Option 2: Pivot to Industrial PPE (Recommended)
- **Pros:** Flame-resistant coveralls, chemical suits (higher margin, stable demand)
- **Cons:** Need new certifications (6-9 months), marketing effort
- **Investment:** IDR 3B (re-tooling)
- **Projected Revenue (2025):** IDR 25B (breakeven)
- **Score:** 7.5/10 ✅

### Option 3: Maintain Status Quo (Current Path)
- **Pros:** No disruption, keep certifications active
- **Cons:** Continued losses, capacity waste
- **Projected Revenue (2025):** IDR 15B (still declining)
- **Score:** 5/10

### Option 4: Premium Fashion Masks (Niche)
- **Pros:** [Batik Tradisional (Traditional Batik)](batik-tradisional.md) print reusable masks, premium pricing
- **Cons:** Small market (IDR 5B max potential)
- **Investment:** Minimal (use existing [Cetak Batik Digital (Digital Batik Printing)](cetak-batik-digital.md))
- **Score:** 6/10

## Social Impact (The "Hidden" Benefit)

### Jobs Preserved (2020-2021)
- **Zero Layoffs** during pandemic (1,200 workers retained)
- **Union Relations:** Strengthened ([Serikat Pekerja PT Maju Bersama](serikat-pekerja.md) praises management)
- **Community Impact:** Supplied 5 million masks to Ministry of Health ([Pivot PPE 2020 — COVID-19 Response Case Study](pivot-ppe-2020.md))

### Skills Developed
- ISO 13485:2016 certification (medical device quality management)
- Clean room operations (transferable to [Tekstil Teknis (Technical Textiles)](tekstil-teknis.md))
- Compliance culture (FDA, CE Marking) → helps [Divisi Ekspor](divisi-ekspor.md) EU market

## Market Reality Check (2023)

| Factor | Status | Trend |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Global PPE Demand | Post-pandemic normalization | ↓↓ Declining |
| Chinese Competition | Dominating low-end (40% price advantage) | ↑ Worsening |
| Indonesian Healthcare Demand | Stable (govt procurement) | → Flat |
| Premium/Niche Demand | Growing (sustainable, fashion masks) | ↑ Emerging |

**Conclusion:** Mass-market PPE is dead for Indonesian manufacturers. Niche/industrial is the only viable path.

## [Ahmad Darmawan](ahmad-darmawan.md)'s Production Assessment

**Current View (2023):**
- "We overbuilt capacity. Should have stopped at 1.5M units/year (not 2.4M)."
- "Industrial PPE is the way forward. Medical PPE is a sunset product line."
- "Keep the clean room. It's transferable to [Tekstil Teknis (Technical Textiles)](tekstil-teknis.md) coating lines."

## Decision Quality for 2020 Pivot: 9/10
**Why not 10/10?** Should have planned exit strategy earlier (2021 vs 2022) and capped capacity at 1.5M units.

## 2024 Recommendation: Pivot to Industrial PPE (Option 2)

1. **Re-tooling (Q1-Q2 2024):** IDR 3B investment in flame-resistant coating
2. **Certification (Q2-Q4 2024):** SNI for industrial PPE, ISO 11612 (heat/fire)
3. **Marketing (2024-2025):** Target mining, shipyard, construction sectors
4. **Target:** IDR 25B revenue by 2025 (breakeven), IDR 35B by 2026 (profitable)
